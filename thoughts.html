Thought Process

How I think through work: diagnose, structure, execute, improve.

A concise view of the reasoning habits I use during consulting, operations, AI-enabled productivity, and continuous learning.

Work reasoning

The mental checkpoints I use while working.

When advising

I connect recommendations to an operating path.

I do not treat research as finished until the recommendation has stakeholders, process ownership, constraints, and next steps attached. My strongest consulting work sits where market logic connects to practical execution.

When improving operations

I look for process fixes people will actually adopt.

Templates, validation checks, digitised records, and clear SOPs only matter if they reduce friction in daily work. I look for changes that make coordination easier without adding unnecessary complexity.

When using AI

I frame the problem first, then use AI to accelerate the work.

I use AI tools for drafting, research, automation, and code assistance, but the value comes from setting the right question, checking outputs, and turning useful results into a practical workflow.

When learning

I use technical learning to sharpen business execution.

Auditing CS50 and management consulting coursework helps me connect business analysis with better data, systems, and implementation thinking.
